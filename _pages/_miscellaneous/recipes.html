The ultimate chocolate cake

I love chocolate cakes. I try to make one for each birthday. For a really long time (and I probably mean for the last ten years) I have been failing at making them as I wanted, i.e., not too sweet, crispy at the top and slightly melting at the bottom. After years of experimenting, here's the ultimate recipe:

Ingredients

  • 200g chocolate (I take it with 70% cocoa)
  • 100g butter
  • 60g flour
  • 3 eggs
  • 10g of sugar (literally)

Steps

Preheat the oven in fan mode at 180°C.

Grease a plate (I have a 20cm-squared glass one).

Break the chocolate in tiny pieces.

Dice the butter in small cubes.

In a double boiler, melt the chocolate.

When the chocolate is melted, add small pieces of butter, one after another.

In the meantime, whip up the eggs (combined, yolk and white together) until the preparation triples in volume.

When all the butter is incorporated, add the flour to the chocolate preparation (this is key!).

Combine the two preparations by adding the chocolate mixture to the eggs. Mix very delicately until the preparation is homogenous. Avoid overmixing to prevent the eggs from collapsing.

Pour the preparation into the greased plate.

Bake for 20 minutes, or until you can slide a knife in and it comes out clean.

You're done!

Parfait à la rhubarbe et coulis à la framboise (Rhubarb Parfait with Raspberry Coulis)

I created this recipe after a bit of trial and error. I am particularly proud of it because it's possible to make it in batch and freeze it. It's a bit advanced, but if you follow the steps carefully, it will be worth it! Aside from the usual cooking utensils, you will need a cooking thermometer.

Ingredients

  • 500g rhubarb
  • 100g raspberries
  • 20cL cream (liquid)
  • 3 eggs
  • 50g water
  • 100g sugar

Steps

    In two separate pots, melt the raspberry and the rhubarb, at low temperature. Stir occasionally. When the preparation is smooth, let it cool at room temperature.

    In the meantime, in a cold bowl, whip up the cream in a chantilly. You can add a bit of sugar & vanilla. Once done, let it cool at room temperature.

    Sabayon

    With the eggs, the sugar and the water, prepare a sabayon:

    Put the water and the sugar in a small pan and heat until 118°C (this is why we need a thermometer!).

    In the meantime, whisk the eggs in a beater until the preparation doubles in volume and becomes whiter.

    Once the sugar + water has reached 118°C add slowly to the eggs while still beating. The idea is that the heated sugar will "cook" the preparation.

    Continue whipping the preparation until it cools down (you can touch the bowl to get an idea).

    When all the (i) rhubarb (ii) whipped cream and (iii) sabayon preparations are roughly the same temperature, combine together and mix delicately so that the sabayon does not fall down

    Staging:

    Put the preparation in a piping bag with your favourite 1cm nozzle.

You're done!

Italian style hot chocolate

To warm up my winters, I love to make this hot chocolate recipe, as I found it in Torino! This recipe makes a very thick hot chocolate, almost like a chocolate sauce! Really filling.

Ingredients for 1 person

  • 30g cocoa powder
  • 10g cornstarch
  • 5g sugar
  • 1 glass of milk

Steps

In a hot pot, add the cocoa powder, the cornstarch and the sugar. Mix very delicately.

When the dry preparation is homogenous, carefully add in the milk.

Turn on the heat to a high temperature. Mix continuously until the preparation thickens.

Do not bring to a boil!

Add some whipped cream if you're keen!
